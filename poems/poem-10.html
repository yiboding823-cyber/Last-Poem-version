行路难·其一

Hard Is the Road · I

李白Li Bai · 唐代Tang Dynasty
金樽清酒斗十千，
玉盘珍羞直万钱。
停杯投箸不能食，
拔剑四顾心茫然。
欲渡黄河冰塞川，
将登太行雪满山。
长风破浪会有时，
直挂云帆济沧海。
Gold goblets of clear wine worth ten thousand coins;
Jade plates of rare delights worth more than all one owns.
I set down cup and chopsticks — cannot eat;
I draw my sword and look around — defeat.
I'd cross the Yellow River — ice blocks the way;
I'd climb the Taihang Mountains — snow fills the day.
The day will come when winds will break the waves apart;
I'll raise cloud-sails and cross the sea with hopeful heart.
作者简介About the Poet

李白才华横溢，却一生仕途坎坷。此诗以「太行山」之难险，比人生之艰，最终以壮志结尾。

Li Bai was supremely gifted yet perpetually thwarted in his official career. Written after being politely dismissed from the imperial court, the poem uses the impassable Taihang Mountains to represent life's obstacles — yet closes with an unbreakable declaration of hope.

创作背景Historical Context

天宝三年（744），李白被唐玄宗赐金放还，政治理想破灭，写下此组诗。以黄河冰封、太行雪山象征仕途险阻。

In 744 CE, Li Bai was graciously dismissed from the Tang imperial court — his political dreams shattered. In this poem, the ice-blocked Yellow River and snow-covered Taihang Mountains stand for every obstacle that blocks a person of talent and vision.

艺术特色Literary Style

全诗情感跌宕起伏，由宴饮之乐转为心茫然，再以太行、黄河写困境，末句突然振起，以豪迈收尾，体现李白不屈的精神。

The poem swings dramatically from feasting to despair, from paralysis to mountains blocking the way — then suddenly soars in its final two lines. The ending is one of the great emotional reversals in Chinese poetry: from defeat to unvanquishable resolve.
