登高

Ascending the Heights

杜甫Du Fu · 唐代Tang Dynasty
风急天高猿啸哀，
渚清沙白鸟飞回。
无边落木萧萧下，
不尽长江滚滚来。
万里悲秋常作客，
百年多病独登台。
艰难苦恨繁霜鬓，
潦倒新停浊酒杯。
Wind sharp, sky vast, apes wail their grief;
White sands, clear isles, birds wheel for relief.
Endless leaves fall in rustling showers;
The endless Yangtze rolls and roars.
Far from home, in autumn's sadness always;
Old and ill, I climb alone these days.
Hardship whitens my temples with frost;
Even my cup of wine — now lost.
作者简介About the Poet

杜甫晚年流寓夔州，病痛缠身，仍登高望远，感天地之浩渺，悲身世之飘零。

In his final years, Du Fu lived in exile in Kuizhou. Ill and weary, he still climbed the heights to contemplate the vast universe and mourn his own drifting fate.

创作背景Historical Context

大历二年（767）重阳节，杜甫登夔州高台，面对秋江，百感交集，写下此诗，被誉为「古今七律第一」。

On the Double Ninth Festival of 767 CE, Du Fu climbed a high platform in Kuizhou. Facing the autumn river, overwhelmed with feeling, he wrote what many consider the greatest seven-character regulated verse in all of Chinese poetry.

艺术特色Literary Style

全诗八句皆对，颔联以「萧萧」与「滚滚」两组叠字，将时间之流逝与自然之壮阔融为一体，气势震撼。

All eight lines form perfect couplets — a rare technical feat. The reduplicative words 'rustling rustling' and 'rolling rolling' fuse the flow of time with the grandeur of nature into one overwhelming, cascading force.
