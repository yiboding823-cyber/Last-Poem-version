望岳

Gazing at Mount Tai

杜甫Du Fu · 唐代Tang Dynasty
岱宗夫如何？
齐鲁青未了。
造化钟神秀，
阴阳割昏晓。
荡胸生曾云，
决眦入归鸟。
会当凌绝顶，
一览众山小。
How grand is Mount Tai, the father peak?
Green rolls across both Qi and Lu to seek.
Creation's wonders gather here alone;
Yin and yang split dusk and dawn in stone.
Great clouds arise to fill and stir the breast;
Eyes stretched wide to catch birds homeward pressed.
One day I'll scale the very highest rise,
And see all lesser mountains shrink in size.
作者简介About the Poet

杜甫（712–770），字子美，被誉为诗圣，与李白并称「李杜」。

Du Fu (712–770), styled Zimei, is revered as the Sage of Poetry and paired with Li Bai as the twin pillars of Tang verse.

创作背景Historical Context

此诗作于唐玄宗开元二十四年（736），杜甫漫游齐赵时，年仅二十四岁。望泰山而生凌云壮志，以山喻志，气势磅礴。

Written in 736 CE when Du Fu was only 24, during his travels through Qi and Zhao. Gazing at Mount Tai, he expressed soaring ambition, using the mountain as a metaphor for the heights he aspired to reach.

艺术特色Literary Style

全诗以问开篇，由远及近，层层推进。颔联写山之神秀，颈联写登山之感，尾联以志气作结，成为千古励志名句。

The poem opens with a question and advances from distant view to close encounter. The neck couplet captures the awe of the climb; the final couplet transforms the landscape into a declaration of personal ambition — one of the most quoted lines of resolve in all of Chinese literature.
