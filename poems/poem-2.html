题西林壁

Inscribed on West Forest Temple Wall

苏轼Su Shi · 宋代Song Dynasty
横看成岭侧成峰，
远近高低各不同。
不识庐山真面目，
只缘身在此山中。
Seen head-on a ridge; from the side a peak;
Far and near, high and low — each view unique.
Why can't I see Mount Lu's true face?
Because I dwell within its embrace.
作者简介About the Poet

苏轼（1037–1101），号东坡居士，北宋文豪，诗词书画无所不精。

Su Shi (1037–1101), known as Su Dongpo, was a supreme polymath of the Song dynasty — master of poetry, prose, calligraphy, and painting.

创作背景Historical Context

宋神宗元丰七年，苏轼由黄州赴汝州途中，游庐山，题于西林寺壁。山形变幻启发哲思，借景论道。

In 1084, while traveling from Huangzhou to Ruzhou, Su Shi visited Mount Lu and inscribed this poem on the wall of West Forest Temple. The ever-shifting silhouette of the mountain sparked a philosophical insight.

艺术特色Literary Style

前两句写庐山形态万变，后两句由景入理，揭示旁观者清的人生哲理，是中国哲理诗的经典代表。

The first two lines describe the mountain's myriad appearances; the last two pivot from scenery to wisdom. The poem is a cornerstone of Chinese philosophical verse, illustrating how our perspective shapes — and limits — our understanding of reality.
